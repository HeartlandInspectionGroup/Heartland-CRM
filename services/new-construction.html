Quality Assurance

Why New Construction Needs Independent Inspection

🏗 Catch Issues Early

Problems are easier and cheaper to fix during construction. Once drywall goes up, defects become hidden and expensive. Our phase inspections catch issues when they're still accessible.

📋 Thorough Quality Review

Municipal inspectors have limited time and focus on basic requirements. We provide a detailed, independent assessment of workmanship and quality — from framing details to finish work — giving you a fuller picture of your home's construction.

⚖️ Warranty Protection

Document any defects before your builder's warranty expires. Many warranty issues must be reported within the first year. Our inspection creates a record of existing conditions.

🔍 Independent Eyes

Your builder's inspector works for the builder. We work for you. An independent inspection gives you unbiased information about the quality of construction.

💰 Avoid Costly Surprises

A $275 Pre-Pour inspection can prevent a $15,000 foundation problem. Phase inspections are the most cost-effective quality assurance you can buy during construction.

📊 Documentation Trail

Every phase inspection produces a detailed report with photos. This creates a permanent record of construction quality and any issues found at each stage.

Inspection Details

What's Inspected at Each Phase

🔩 Pre-Pour Phase

  • Footing placement and dimensions
  • Rebar sizing, spacing, and placement
  • Anchor bolt positioning
  • Soil compaction and grading
  • Drainage tile installation
  • Vapor barrier placement

🪵 Pre-Drywall Phase

  • Wall framing, headers, and structural connections
  • Floor joist spacing and sizing
  • Roof truss installation
  • Electrical wiring and box placement
  • Plumbing rough-in and venting
  • HVAC ductwork routing
  • Insulation type and coverage
  • Fire blocking and draft stopping
  • Window and door flashing

🏠 Final Walkthrough

  • All finished surfaces and workmanship
  • Appliance installation and operation
  • HVAC system testing
  • Plumbing fixtures and water flow
  • Electrical outlets, switches, and GFCI
  • Doors, windows, and hardware
  • Grading and drainage around foundation
  • Exterior caulking and weatherproofing
  • Smoke/CO detector placement and function
  • Garage door safety features

📦 Foundation to Finish (All 3)

  • Everything above, inspected at the right time
  • Continuity between phases — we track issues from foundation through completion
  • Final punch list for builder negotiation
